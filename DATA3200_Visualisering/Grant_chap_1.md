# Why visualize?
* Busy readers need simple visualizations to *efficiently understand data*

### Pros of visualization
* Compact
* Accessible
* Top message quickly conveyed
* Eye-catching / exciting
* Actionable insights derived from complex data.
* Shows the whole picture at a glance
* Easier anomaly-detection
### The brain
is built to detect:
* Patterns
* Differences
* Spacial relationships

concerning physical things as opposed to abstract representations (datasets).

The more complex the data, the truer this gets. Datasets with only a few datapoints seldom call for visual representation.

### Layering information
in a hierarchical way from less complex headline-visualizations to increasingly complex tables can be useful, enabling keener readers to deep-dive if they wish.

### Gelman & Unwin's 6 reasons for data visualization:
Non interperative reasons:
1. to give an overview
2. to show the scale and complexity of the data
3. to allow exploration of data

Communicating a message:

4. to communicate findings
5. to tell a story
6. to attract attention and stimulate interest

""Being deliberate in your choice of communication is not nessecarily antonymous to scientific objectivity.""
